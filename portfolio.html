Python Graph

In this project, we took a specific set of data off the internet on the number of deaths caused by natural disasters by year. The data was then put through python code that reads the data and transforms it into a scatterplot, making the data easier and more efficient to read. The y-axis is the number of deaths and the x-axis is the number of years from 1980. The question answered by the graph is whether or not the number decreased due to better preparation throughout the years


NetLogo Remix

For this project, we picked a simulation for the spreading of HIV within a population. This example answers the question of how fast HIV spreads within a population and how many people are aware that they are infected. We modified the code to change the infected people who dont know that they have the disease to yellow instead of blue. Additionally,we changed the rate of infection from 50% to 27%, the initial number of people infected from 25 to 20, and symptoms of the disase arising from 200 weeks to 4 weeks to make it more realistic.
